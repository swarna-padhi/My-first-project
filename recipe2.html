Mango lassi

Description

mango lassi is a refreshing, creamy Indian beverage made by blending sweet mangoes with yogurt. It’s essentially a fruity variation of the traditional Punjabi lassi and is perfect for cooling down during hot summer months or balancing a spicy meal.

ingridient

  • Mango: 1 cup of chopped ripe mango (fresh or frozen) or mango pulp. Alphonso or Kesar varieties are highly recommended for their rich colour and sweetness.
  • Yogurt: 1 cup of plain, thick whole-milk yogurt (Indian curd/dahi works best).
  • Liquid: ½ cup of cold milk or water to adjust the thickness.
  • Sweetener: 1.5 to 3 tablespoons of sugar or honey (adjust based on the mango's sweetness).
  • Flavouring (Optional): ¼ teaspoon of ground cardamom and a few strands of saffron.
  • Ice: 3–4 ice cubes if you prefer it extra cold and thick.

step 1

Prepare the Mango: If using fresh fruit, wash, peel, and chop the mango into small cubes. For an even better texture, some recipes suggest freezing the mango chunks for 30 minutes before blending

step 2

Combine Ingredients: Add the mango pieces (or pulp), yogurt, milk/water, sugar, and cardamom powder into a high-speed blender.

step 3

Blend: Blend on high speed for about 1 minute until the mixture is perfectly smooth and frothy.

step 4

Adjust Consistency: Check the thickness. If it’s too thick, add a splash more milk or water and blend for a few seconds. If you want it sweeter, add a bit more sugar.

step 5

Serve: Pour the lassi into tall glasses. Garnish with a pinch of saffron strands, chopped pistachios, or a few small mango cubes on top. Serve immediately while chilled.
